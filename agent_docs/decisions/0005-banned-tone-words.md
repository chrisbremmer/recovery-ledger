# 0005. Banned tone words

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-05-12
- **Decider(s):** CB

## Context

The review output is the product's voice. A WHOOP-data review that
reads like wellness-app marketing — "optimize your recovery!", "crush
this week's journey!", "🔥 unlock your peak!" — defeats the entire
premise of the project, which is to treat the data as input to a
direct, non-hype decision loop.

Three concrete failure modes the rule prevents:

1. **Anthropomorphising the data.** "Your body is telling you…" — no,
   the data shows a pattern.
2. **Moralising fatigue.** "Honor your rest day" — sleep debt is a
   signal, not a moral failure.
3. **Hype words for normal outcomes.** "Optimize", "unlock", "dial in",
   "tune", "vibe" — these turn a measurement into a sales pitch.

## Decision

**The following words are banned in any review output**, JSON or text,
generated by `src/formatters/` or rendered by the CLI / MCP surfaces:

```
optimize
wellness
honor
journey
crush
nail
dial in
tune
vibe
unlock
```

**All emoji are banned** in review output. (Emoji in commit messages,
PR descriptions, or comments to humans are fine — this rule is about
the rendered review, not the codebase generally.)

Replacement guidance:

- "Optimize recovery" → "Do Zone 2 or mobility today."
- "Honor your sleep debt" → "Sleep-debt signal, not a moral failure."
- "Unlock your peak" → "Strain ceiling is around X today."

The rule does not apply to error messages, log lines, or internal
diagnostics — but if a banned word appears there too, it usually
means we're soft-selling the data internally.

## Consequences

- The renderer is forced to make claims directly. "Do X today" is
  shorter than "consider whether you might want to optimize toward X."
- Tone is testable: the same string-match rule that bans the words in
  CI also bans them in fixtures and snapshot tests.
- Adding a new word is one line in the lint config plus a row in this
  ADR's history.

## Alternatives considered

- **Discourage in style guide, enforce by review.** Rejected: the
  failure is too easy to commit; CI gate is cheap.
- **LLM-judge tone-scorer.** Rejected: slow, non-deterministic, and
  the banned-word list catches 90% of failures for zero cost.

## Enforcement

- Biome / regex CI step fails the build if any banned word (case-
  insensitive, word-boundary) appears in `src/formatters/`,
  `src/cli/`, or test fixtures under `tests/fixtures/review/`.
- The same script flags any emoji codepoint (U+1F000–U+1FFFF,
  U+2600–U+27BF) in the same paths.
- Contract test that runs the renderer on every fixture and re-checks
  the rendered output for banned tokens (defence in depth — catches
  generated content, not just source).
